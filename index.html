Random Words:

There are so many emotions that sometimes you just can’t find a word to describe it. But maybe you just haven’t been looking hard enough. Languages across the world have words that are untranslatable for this very reason. In Portuguese, the word “saudade” stands for that feeling when you miss someone you loved fiercely.
Vellichor - the wistfulness you feel when you’re in a second-hand bookstore.
Opia - the somehow both vulnerable and invasive intensity that you feel when looking someone dead in the eye.
Ellipsism - a sadness that you’ll never be able to see how history turns out.
Kenopsia - the eerie, forlorn atmosphere of a place that is normally bustling and full of people but is now abandoned and silent.
Sonder - the realization that everyone around you has a life just as vivid and complex as yours.
Liberosis - the desire to care less about things.
Rubatosis - the unsettling awareness of your own heartbeat.

Video Games:

As the evolution of video games progressed, so did its history. It was rumored to be a government-built machine made to take control of people's minds.
